Meet the Couple

ExaGingers

Our story has been that of LOVE, FUN, FRIENDSHIP and GROWTH. We believe our coming together is launching us unto our EXA-LIFE. We look forward to see great things the next phase unfolds.

Read our individual stories below.

Kikelomo Adesayo

While I was at the College of Engineering at FUTA back in Nigeria, I had recognized Victor to be one of the students at my College. I didn't even know his name. I knew a little about him when he was about leaving for the US to FAMU. We became friends after I joined our cohort in January 2015.

I would always stay at the College Library to study overnight and I guess that was a major attraction for him. LOL. He was curious about how I had so much energy and was very firm in my studies. At some point, he started coming over to study and we would take breaks and talk about life values, goals, our faith and many other things. May 2nd, he told me how much he liked me and he wanted me to be his girlfriend and promised he was going to marry me (marriage ke, I thought in my mind) but I saw all symptoms of seriousness and told him, I will think and pray about it (Church girl, in Jenifa's accent). To cut the long story short, after 2 months, I gave him a yes and here we are!

Tolulope Victor

I knew Kikelomo as an Engineering student in 2011 because we had few female students in the school of engineering but everything I knew about her was her name and her department. Fast forwarded to 2014, we were both involved in an exchange program and ten students made it by God’s grace and our families’ support. I was able to identify tenacity, dedication, hope, faith, and love in Kikelomo throughout the transition process. After we got to the US, I decided to be personally helpful to her in any way I could be helpful. Then, I became her friend. Being her friend is one of the most important things that led us to where we are and kept us together throughout this journey.

Our friendship became so serious that we shared deep things together, but most importantly we started sharing our dreams with each other, our life goals, our passion, career goals and paths, and our faith of an extraordinary life. Sharing our future destination revealed the ultimate need for each other to achieve them.

I was not ready for a relationship, so asking her out was not an option, at least then. One day, during my devotion, God revealed to me that she is MY MISSING RIB and that it was time to ask her out. I did it and here we are, Moving on to our Exa-Married life.
